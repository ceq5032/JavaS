Early Life and Jedi Training

Anakin Skywalker is born on the desert planet of Tatooine.

Year: -41 BBY

Tatooine orbited twin suns, Tatoo I and Tatoo II. According to Jawa folklore, the desert known as the Dune Sea was once a true ocean. The whole planet was covered in harsh deserts, and only a small part of its northern hemisphere could sustain intelligent life.

Discovered by Jedi Master Qui-Gon Jinn and taken to the Jedi Temple

Year: -32 BBY

The Jedi Temple is a central location on Coruscant in the Star Wars universe, serving as the headquarters of the Jedi Order. It functions as a training ground for young Force-sensitive individuals, houses the Jedi High Council, and contains vast archives and holocrons of knowledge. Its design reflects the Jedi's values of peace and discipline.

Becomes a Jedi Knight under the tutelage of Obi-Wan Kenobi.

Year: -22 BBY

Anakin Skywalker’s training as a Jedi was unconventional and marked by both his exceptional skills and emotional struggles.

The Clone Wars and the Fall to the Dark Side

Falls in love with Senator Padmé Amidala and secretly marries her.

Year: -22 BBY

Anakin Skywalker and Padmé Amidala first met when Anakin was a young boy on Tatooine in The Phantom Menace. Padmé, then Queen of Naboo, was stranded on the planet after her ship was damaged. Anakin, a slave, helped her and her crew repair the ship. Despite their age difference, there was an immediate sense of connection. Anakin, who was discovered by Jedi Master Qui-Gon Jinn, later reunites with Padmé when she becomes a senator, and their bond deepens over time, eventually leading to a secret romance and marriage.

Experiences visions of Padmé's death and seeks help from the enigmatic Darth Sidious.

Year: -19 BBY

Anakin Skywalker begins having visions of Padmé's death during childbirth, which causes him intense fear. Desperate to save her, he turns to Darth Sidious, who promises him the power to cheat death through the dark side of the Force, ultimately leading Anakin to betray the Jedi and become Darth Vader.

Turns to the dark side of the Force and becomes Darth Vader.

Year: -19 BBY

Anakin Skywalker turns to the dark side of the Force after being manipulated by Darth Sidious (Chancellor Palpatine), who promises him the power to prevent Padmé’s death. Consumed by fear and anger, Anakin abandons the Jedi and becomes **Darth Vader**, a Sith Lord, ultimately betraying his friends and the Jedi Order.

Leads the attack on the Jedi Temple, killing many younglings.

Year: -19 BBY

Anakin, now Darth Vader, leads the attack on the Jedi Temple as part of Order 66, killing many Jedi, including younglings, in his effort to eliminate the Jedi Order and cement his loyalty to the Sith.

Battles Obi-Wan Kenobi on Mustafar, resulting in severe injuries.

Year: -19 BBY

Anakin, now Darth Vader, battles his former mentor Obi-Wan Kenobi on Mustafar. The fight ends with Anakin suffering severe injuries, leaving him severely burned and near death.

The Reign of the Empire

Becomes the Emperor's enforcer and a symbol of the Galactic Empire's power.

Year: -19 BBY

Anakin, now Darth Vader, becomes the Emperor's enforcer, serving as a feared symbol of the Galactic Empire's power and enforcing its rule across the galaxy.

Hunts down remaining Jedi and rebels, including his former apprentice, Luke Skywalker.

Year: -0 BBY

Darth Vader hunts down the remaining Jedi and rebels after the rise of the Galactic Empire, serving as the Emperor’s enforcer. His search includes hunting his own son, Luke Skywalker, unaware that Luke is his child. Vader's role is crucial in suppressing the Rebel Alliance, but his actions are driven by loyalty to the Emperor and a belief in the Sith's vision of galactic control, even as he remains conflicted about his past.

Redemption and Sacrifice

Confronts his son, Luke Skywalker, and is ultimately redeemed.

Year: 4 ABY

In Return of the Jedi, Darth Vader confronts his son, Luke Skywalker, trying to convince him to join the dark side and rule the galaxy together. .

Sacrifices himself to save Luke and destroy the Emperor.

Year: 4 ABY

Their duel intensifies, but Luke refuses to kill his father, and Vader, torn by his internal conflict, ultimately chooses to redeem himself by sacrificing himself to save Luke and destroy the Emperor
